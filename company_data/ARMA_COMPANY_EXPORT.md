ARMA Company Export

This file is a consolidated export of current knowledge about Arma Automotive Inc. based on the working markdown notes in this workspace.

It is intended as a clean snapshot rather than a brainstorming document.


Company Overview

Arma Automotive Inc. was started in 2020 in Canada with the original goal of building kit cars.

The company built a prototype vehicle that is on the road. The prototype required multiple years of work and the effort of several people. It is not commercial quality, but it produced substantial learning and led to what is believed to be about 95% of a production-ready kit-car design.

The current business direction is not to become a full vehicle manufacturer immediately. The current focus is to generate revenue through CNC fabrication tools, while keeping the car program alive in a lower-risk form.


Current Strategic Focus

Current priority order:

1. Sell the portable CNC tube notcher
2. Productize and later sell the CNC tube bender kit
3. Use ADS software as a support product for the CNC tools
4. Keep the kit-car program alive through plans, molds, and possible builder licensing

The main objective for the current year is to break even using CNC tube notcher sales.


Business Model Direction

The strongest near-term business model is:

1. Sell CNC fabrication tools
2. Support builders who use those tools
3. Use software, plans, molds, and documentation to strengthen the tool ecosystem

The company is currently better positioned as an enabling platform for builders rather than as a primary vehicle OEM.


Current Company Assets

Core assets currently identified:

1. Portable CNC tube notcher
2. CNC tube bender kit
3. ADS CAD/CAM software
4. Kit-car design
5. Body molds and 5-axis router capability
6. Prototype vehicle and accumulated know-how


Revenue Priorities

Primary revenue priority:

1. Portable CNC tube notcher sales

Secondary revenue priority:

1. CNC tube bender kit once sufficiently productized

Support-product revenue:

1. ADS CAD/CAM software, primarily as an included or supporting product rather than a standalone growth line

Potential low-priority/adjacent revenue:

1. Builder licensing for the car
2. Plans, molds, and related builder support
3. Brand products such as model kits


Current Pricing Direction

Portable CNC tube notcher:

1. Standard price: $12,000 USD
2. Launch price: $10,500 USD for the first 5 units

Internal shipping note:

The company may internally budget about $400 CAD per unit as part of shipping absorption logic, while still quoting shipping separately to the customer by region or destination.


Sales Direction

Current sales direction is based on direct outreach rather than depending primarily on ads.

The current view is that this type of product is more likely to sell through:

1. Direct outreach
2. Proof assets such as videos
3. Qualification calls
4. Structured follow-up
5. A clear sales funnel

Advertising may support awareness, but is not treated as the main acquisition engine at this stage.


Key Customer Segments

Highest-value current segments:

1. Custom car builders
2. Motorsport and roll cage shops
3. Off-road and overland fabricators
4. Metal fabricators doing repeat tube work

Secondary or lower-priority segments:

1. Universities
2. Retailers


Operating Structure

Known operating resources:

1. Shop space in Victoria, BC
2. One employee
3. A co-op expected through the summer

Current role allocation direction:

1. Founder focuses on sales, outreach, demos, and customer discovery
2. Engineer/employee supports assembly, testing, QA, and product improvements
3. Co-op supports assembly, documentation, packaging, labels, and bounded secondary productization work


Known Cost Structure Notes

Known recurring costs discussed so far:

1. Shop rent: about $5,000 CAD per month
2. One employee: about $6,000 CAD per month

This implies at least about $11,000 CAD per month in base recurring cost before other items such as marketing, founder compensation, legal, insurance, shipping, and materials outside unit-level assumptions.


Launch Readiness Priorities

Launch-critical work for the notcher includes:

1. Assembly repeatability
2. Calibration process
3. Manual and documentation
4. Product stickers and warning labels
5. Packaging and shipping approach
6. Support readiness
7. Clear product limitations and buyer expectations


Current Weekly Execution Mindset

The dominant strategic conclusion from the planning work is that the company does not currently need a new direction. It needs faster execution on the current direction.

The current problem is not lack of ideas. It is finishing, shipping, selling, and learning from real buyers.


ADS Software Positioning

Current view of ADS:

1. It likely does not compete well enough on its own against products such as Fusion 360
2. It should be treated as a support product for the CNC tools
3. It remains valuable because it strengthens the machine ecosystem and differentiates the tools
4. It should not currently be treated as a primary standalone business line

AI-assisted product-generation ideas are considered interesting but technically uncertain and not validated enough to justify current focus.


Kit-Car Program

The car remains strategically important, but it is not the main business focus for the current period.

Current view:

1. Building cars directly is too capital-intensive and risky right now
2. Builder licensing, plans, molds, and related support may be a better near-term way to preserve and monetize the asset
3. The car can also function as a flagship/design halo product for the broader tool ecosystem


Brand and Merch Direction

A 3D-printed model kit of the car is viewed as a possible brand/marketing product rather than a true profit center.

The purpose would be:

1. Brand awareness
2. Giving people something tangible to buy now
3. Priming the revenue pump
4. Keeping the car visible before full vehicle availability

The current concept is for unfinished kits that the customer assembles, glues, and paints themselves, reducing labour burden on the company.


Longer-Term Roadmap Direction

The most logical future product family after the current notcher and bender kit appears to be a higher-end notcher variant or v2.

Potential future directions discussed:

1. A non-portable higher-end notcher
2. Support for square or rectangular tube
3. Support for angle/L-section
4. Another rotational axis or more advanced geometry capability
5. More industrial-duty architecture for higher-end buyers

The current conclusion is still to avoid broadening the roadmap until the current products are validated in the market.


International Sales Direction

For US sales, the company should not absorb tariffs, duties, or import-side charges by default.

Current direction:

1. Buyer is responsible for tariffs, duties, brokerage, and import-side costs
2. These responsibilities should be stated early in the sales process and in formal sales documents
3. Cross-border costs and shipping should not be left ambiguous


Overall Company Thesis

The company appears best positioned as:

1. A CNC fabrication-tool company
2. A builder-enablement company
3. A design-and-manufacturing ecosystem around tube structures, vehicles, and related products

It is not currently best positioned as a full integrated vehicle manufacturer.


Snapshot Conclusion

Current best move:

1. Finish the notcher launch
2. Start direct sales
3. Learn from real buyers
4. Productize the bender in controlled downtime
5. Keep the car and software as strategic assets, not distractions
