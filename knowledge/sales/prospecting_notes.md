# Prospecting Notes

Ideal prospect characteristics:
- Companies doing repeated tube cutting, coping, or welded tube assembly work
- Shops where throughput, repeatability, or labor efficiency matters
- Businesses where poor fit-up causes rework or schedule delays

Buying triggers to watch for:
- Hiring growth in fabrication or welding roles
- Expansion into production work
- New product lines using tube structures
- Public emphasis on reducing lead times or increasing output
- Signs of manual or low-automation fabrication workflows

Questions the prospecting agent should answer:
- What does the company manufacture?
- How likely are they to fabricate tube assemblies?
- What pain might the CNC tube notcher remove?
- Who might own the buying decision?
- Why is this prospect a fit or not a fit?

Disqualification signals:
- No meaningful tube fabrication work
- Pure design/resale business with little in-house manufacturing
- Very small one-off custom work with weak repeatability needs
