# Market Categories

## Strongest near-term buyer categories
- Off-road / 4x4 fabrication shops
- Automotive fabrication shops
- Chassis builders
- Roll cage builders
- Motorsport / race fabrication shops
- Tube fabrication shops doing repeat coping/notching work
- Metal product manufacturers producing welded tube frames, racks, guards, or similar tubular assemblies

## Broader fabrication and manufacturing categories
- OEMs with in-house round-tube fabrication
- Contract manufacturers doing repeat welded tube assembly work
- General fabrication shops with repeated round-tube work
- Truck equipment and utility-body fabricators
- Agricultural equipment fabricators using round tube
- Industrial fabricators making tubular assemblies
- Marine fabricators using round tube

## Education and ecosystem categories
- Universities, labs, and student teams building tubular structures
- Tool distributors or channel partners once direct demand is proven

## Typical product applications
- Tube coping / notching for welded joints
- Roll cages
- Tube chassis
- Welded tube frames
- Racks, guards, bumpers, sliders, and tubular accessories
- Repeated fit-up work where labor time and consistency matter
- Part marking / engraving for fabrication guidance

## Best-fit signals within these categories
- Repeated in-house round tube fabrication
- Public evidence of welded tube products or assemblies
- Throughput, lead-time, or labor-efficiency pressure
- Need for better repeatability and fit-up
- Buyer can justify roughly $12,000 USD for labor-saving fabrication equipment

## Lower-priority or conditional categories
- Very general fabrication shops with little visible tube specialization
- Buyers doing mostly one-off custom jobs with low repeatability
- Distributors without proof of channel demand
- Education buyers with long procurement cycles
