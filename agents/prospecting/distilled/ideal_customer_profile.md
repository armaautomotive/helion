# Ideal Customer Profile

## Best-fit company types
- Tube fabrication shops doing repeatable coping/notching work
- Off-road and automotive fabrication shops building roll cages, chassis parts, or tubular assemblies
- Chassis and roll cage builders
- Metal product manufacturers producing welded tube frames, racks, guards, or similar tube assemblies
- OEMs or contract manufacturers with in-house welded round-tube production

## Best-fit production characteristics
- Repeated round tube and pipe fabrication, not just occasional cuts
- In-house welding/fabrication where fit-up quality affects downstream work
- Throughput, repeatability, and labor efficiency matter to margins
- Need for multiple similar parts, fixtures, or production batches
- Shops that would value a compact machine rather than a large permanent installation

## Use cases the product aligns with
- Straight tube cutting
- Tube coping/intersection cuts at angles
- Tab-and-slot style fit-up support
- Pie-cut section production
- Part marking/engraving for labels or bend references

## Likely buyer roles
- Owner / founder of a fabrication shop
- Production manager
- Fabrication manager
- Manufacturing engineer
- Shop lead or operations lead in a tube-heavy workflow

## Commercial fit
- Price direction suggests fit is strongest for businesses that can justify about $12,000 USD for labor-saving fabrication equipment
- Likely best for small-to-mid-size fabrication businesses rather than hobby users
