# Buyer Signals

## Strong fit signals
- Company manufactures welded tube assemblies, frames, cages, racks, chassis parts, or roll structures
- Evidence of repeated in-house round tube fabrication
- Public photos/videos show manual coping, plasma cutting, or other low-automation tube workflows
- Company emphasizes throughput, lead time, production growth, or manufacturing efficiency
- Hiring for welders, fabricators, or production roles
- Expansion into production runs or new tube-based product lines
- Uses round steel or aluminum tube in finished products

## Signals of urgency
- Growing fabrication team or adding shifts
- Complaints or visible friction around fit-up, labor time, or production bottlenecks
- Need for repeatable part marking, cut consistency, or assembly guidance
- Shop space constraints that make portable/compact equipment attractive

## Research questions for qualification
- What tube products do they make?
- Is tube fabrication done in-house?
- Is the work repetitive enough to justify automation?
- Are they using round tube within the supported range?
- Who owns tooling/equipment purchases?
