# Disqualifiers

## Poor-fit company types
- Businesses with no meaningful in-house fabrication
- Pure design, resale, or integration firms without manufacturing
- Shops focused mainly on sheet metal, machining, or non-tube work
- Very small one-off custom shops with weak repeatability needs

## Technical mismatch signals
- Primary use is square, rectangular, angle, or other non-round profiles
- Main work involves solid rod rather than tube/pipe
- Work appears outside the current round-tube size/material boundaries
- Need for a laser-based process or claims beyond practical plasma capability

## Commercial/process disqualifiers
- No evidence that labor efficiency, repeatability, or fit-up quality matters
- Purchase budget clearly far below the expected price range
- Workflow is too low-volume or occasional to justify dedicated tube notching equipment
